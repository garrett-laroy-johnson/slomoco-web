Corpos Sonoros Micro-Residency Journal Report of the 2nd Day - 20/05/2021 (Originally written in Portuguese and then translated to English) João: The second meeting started with a good conversation, updating us on how life is going, the other works and creations we are doing beyond the residency. We started thinking about the practice thinking about how it would be conducted. We decided to keep last week's practice with the focus on investigating the relations of movement and sound, which I proposed to conduct after a similar conduction as last week. The intention would be to focus on the gestures of curving the chest inward with both hands pointing and pressing it (which Thembi selected from last week's improvisation) and the gliding of the hands over the arms and forearms (which I selected last week). We followed the following process: - (5 min) Mapping the body - Drawing on the body sheet the body state before the practice - (15min) Body practice conducted by Thembi - (15min or more) Sound and movement practice conducted by me - (5min) Body mapping - Drawing on the body sheet of the body state after the practice - Conversation about the process, life and everything else I felt a lot of pleasure in the initial 5 minutes of mapping. It feels like my inner space was expanding and building a field that enveloped my whole workshop. I connected with my body and noticed how my lower back was still sore and excessively arched. I noticed that my fingers were locked, like old gears that were moving with difficulty. Unlike last week, I didn't feel anxiety or insecurity in my mind, there was just a noise that quickly faded away, like a cloud blown away by the wind. And my heart was beating very strongly! Thembi's practice started exploring the movement of the hands in contact with each other in relation to the movement of the body with the floor. It was very interesting to perceive the floor in an analogous way to my hand, with slow movements I was noticing how the floor pushed my body and led my movement in an alternating manner with my hand, which also led the movement from where I was touching it. Soon the exploration went deeper, seeking other contacts between different parts of the body, different dynamics, speeds, qualities of contact and movement. The 15 minutes were very long, time seemed to stretch as I investigated new movement possibilities. I loved Thembi's suggestion to notice the difference between the hand leading the movement from where it is touching to when the hand is just gliding across the other moving limb. When she concluded the practice and directed me to lead the sound and movement practice I didn't really know what I was going to do, I just knew I was going to start from the sound of the breath to get to sounds related to the two gestures we chose. Her practice ended with her body lying on the floor and her hands on her heart. I started by exploring deep breathing, how the chest pushes the hands on the inhale and how the hands weigh on the chest on the exhale. Then I shifted my attention to the sound of breathing and I conducted an investigation into what different sounds we could emit when breathing without vocalizing. We breathed through the mouth, blocking the air with our teeth, tongue, lips, even whistling. We also breathed through our nose with different qualities of nostril opening. Still without vocalizing we explored the sounds by investigating what makes the sound deeper, higher, louder and quieter. We then started to improvise the body movement according to the sound of the breath. When there was sound there was movement, and when there was no sound there was no movement. We then started to investigate the relationship between the sound of the breath and the movement with the principle of contact movement between two parts of the body, always trying to keep the movement leading the sound. Gradually I suggested other relations between sound and movement, of the volume of the sound, height being modified by some quality of the movement, researching which movement can conduct which sound. Initially I experimented with analogical relations such as high-pitched sounds making me move my body up and low-pitched sounds moving down. Higher sounds (volume) with faster movements and lower sounds with slower movements. I suggested that we experiment with inverse relationships as well. We started experimenting with the breath sounds being led by one of the gestures defined last week. I started with the one of bending the chest inward while the hands pressed and pointed on it with the fingernails of one hand touching the fingernails of the other hand. We followed both this contracting movement and its opposite expanding movement, with the hands moving away from the chest until we experienced other qualities of contact between the hands. We introduced vocalization into the sound and investigated all the possibilities of movement within that gesture and different qualities of sound related to each part of the movement. After an exploratory improvisation we chose a defined relationship between sound and movement. It was interesting how the process of defining the sound happened intuitively. Since the movement didn't stop for me to choose, it seems that my whole Soma chose what that sound would be, going for a very analogous and direct relationship. We began to consider this mapping between gesture and sound as a musical instrument and started to investigate new sounds that we would "play" with this instrument. As I expanded the possibilities of techniques of my instrument, I created new qualities of sound related to movement. Initially the idea was just to have a low-pitched sound when the hands touched the chest and a high-pitched sound of inspiration when the hand moved away. As I explored new techniques, I made the amount of trunk curvature make the sound deeper or higher in pitch. I also noticed differences in the timbre of the inhaling noise depending on the height that the hands went up, the higher they went, the higher the pitch was. We then dismantled the instruments and laid down for 5 minutes mapping out the state our bodies were in by just staying still on the floor. It was amazing to feel vibrations in my chest beyond the heartbeat, I felt a very intense energy coming out of it. Instead of drawing this wave on the body sheet with the color red, I felt an attraction to draw with a green pen. Later, talking to Thembi, I researched on the internet what the color of the heart chakra was, and to our surprise it was exactly green. Thinking about the practice now, I noticed how the whole of today was centered on the chest and the heart chakra. I felt my heart beating stronger today at the beginning, we finished the first movement practice with both hands on the chest, we started the second practice breathing with both hands on the chest, I intuitively chose between the two gestures the movement of bending and opening the chest... I feel it was quite intense work of this chakra that intuitively guided our work. I though how interesting it could be a practice motivated by a chakra instead of a movement principle. Maybe an emotional state, an image… Interesting possibilities for these Soma-Design practices. Thembi: Today instead of working in the living room of my house, I was in the living room of the casamanga (a cultural center) where it is very spacious and gets delicious sunshine in the afternoon. Since I took the linoleum off the floor and the floor is made of stone, it is very hard and cold. Contrary to the assumptions of the ideal floor for dancing, I have enjoyed moving on this floor. But I noticed that my hands were warm and my feet cold. I set the timer to 5', but when it rang I asked myself if it is really necessary to set this rigid timer time or if it is not better to follow the time we need for this body mapping. Even though I was already starting the movement practice, the alarm clock brought me some of that irritation, of an external stimulus when it wakes us up. Maybe that is not necessary. I love the contact with the floor, so although our focus was on hand contact, I ended up also using body contact with the floor, mapping which parts of the body were touching, sliding across the floor, the different textures of touch from skin to skin, to hair, to the floor, from skin to clothing. The different qualities, textures of touching, squeezing, carrying a body part, what touch can look like with body parts other than hands. How are these contacts between elbow and knee, chin and shoulders, and others. How to follow in this flow of contacts with distinct speeds, accelerating and decelerating as much as possible, the minimum of continuous movement, is a pattern that has always attracted me. When guiding a practice, I sometimes wonder if I am offering multiple stimuli and not allowing time for the other's experience, but I trust that there will always be the option of selecting what is most consistent with one's practice. That leading, moving, saying are intertwined. As if it were a thinking out loud, and yet, it always escapes. So, in this speech there is already a choice of what is more relevant to me, of what gains syntax, and communicates. The time of speech does not accompany the time of thought, which comes with sensations, images, that is, a whole impossible to be totally verbalized. I thought now that we could use EEG to measure brain activity before, during and after the practice. Because I felt, especially with the practice of breathing and vocalizations, how the flow of thoughts slowed down little by little, which is a relief! I've been thinking about our practices and this relationship between wellness, healing, and technology. There is a resistance to being so absorbed by these online contacts, and a questioning of how we take advantage of these technologies for vitality as well, and not just for getting exhausted. This is a big issue that was accentuated with the pandemic and brought us the mandatory of the online, no longer being an object of desire or choice. This changes everything. Even though we were captured long ago, but still with the option of face-to-face meetings and work and not in a health crisis as we have been for over a year now and with no prospect of an end. Katherina mentioned something that drew my attention even more to an aspect that interests me in technology. After this digression, I return to the beginning of the conduction of sounds and movements guided by João. We ended up on the floor with our hands supported on the sternum, and the first stimulus was to focus on breathing, on the sounds emitted by inhaling and exhaling, on the differences between a continuous, chopped, long, brief breath. In the variations between releasing the air through the nose, through the mouth, with the teeth blocking the air, with a more closed mouth. How the movement with the sounds of the breath, with the focus on the sternum, and with connected hands begin to establish. João suggested pauses during inspiration and movement on expiration. In general, it seems to be much easier to make sounds on the exhale, and I felt the movements very connected to how I used to release the air. More continuous, continuous movements, circular, more staccato, chopped movements, with attack. The more this letting out of the air was connected to a pressure on the sternum, the deeper the sound became. Especially as we started with the vocalizations. I felt a need to start the vocalizations, but João suggested we stay a little longer on the blowing, on these sounds of air going in and out. It is a very intense practice reminiscent of the Yoga pranayamas, and it leads to bodily states in which we tend to let our guard down for judgments. That's why I thought it was good to insist on the breaths, because when the vocalizations came in, it seemed to open up an improvisational space and a body state for them to happen more freely and less in terms of composition, aesthetic criteria, they could be more fluid and organic. Other parameters that appeared in the notions of instruments suggested by João: More guttural sounds when spiraling the movement from the connections of the hands on the floor; lower pitched sounds when bending the thorax, ooooo sounds higher pitched sounds when opening the chest in a curve and with the need for an impulsive that called the sound AAAA and more staccato the metaphor of closing (low sounds) and opening (high sounds) seems to be established quite strongly here João suggested we try to invert the logic of what we were doing, but it seems that at the moment I didn't fully understand, and as I felt I was doing many different things, I preferred to try to establish something in the patterns between sounds and movements that were being established. We ended up exhausting the possibilities of this sound proposal with the focus on the external and on the connections with the hands. I also had a very strong sensation in the cardiac plexus, as if radiating from it to the whole body. When I did the ground melting and mapping again, I felt the whole body warmer, but well supported on the ground, with a better circulation of fluids and with the flow of thought much more spaced out. A more relaxed state than in the beginning. After comparing our second drawings, done after the practice, both were done in green. So we found out that the heart chakra is green. It was funny, because in the beginning we talked about aura, because John had the camera in a position to the sun where an aura was drawn over his head. This subject had already come up, and the drawings on the body sheet also refer to the practice of aura studies, mediumistic people, or people who study and can see and draw the aura, as well as works in technology that address these practices. This is not the case, but it is interesting to recognize the similarities between our drawings / sensations. Another thing that has now started to become clearer is the notion of instrument that João has been proposing since the beginning of this research. Only after these two practices in which we started to map the principles of movements and their relations with sounds that it seems to have become clearer a way to think of parameters of the giromin that are established from the practice and not the other way around. It seems to be quite obvious, and although this was quite explicit in the project, without going through the experience it is hard to visualize how to actually establish it.
